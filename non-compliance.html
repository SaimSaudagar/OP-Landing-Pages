Received a Non-Compliance Notice?

OnlyPark is engaged by landlords, property owners and managers to manage parking at privately owned properties.

We issue notices when a vehicle has not complied with the parking terms and conditions displayed onsite, as instructed by the property owner or manager.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Can I appeal a non-compliance notice?
Yes. If you believe the non-compliance notice was issued in error, you can lodge an appeal through the link at the top of this page. Appeals must include relevant details and any supporting documentation.
Why did I receive a non-compliance notice?
01
You received a non-compliance notice because your vehicle was found to be in breach of the parking terms and conditions displayed at the property. This could include parking without a valid permit, overstaying time limits, or parking in unauthorised areas.
How long does it take to receive an outcome?
02
Appeals typically take up to 7 business days to process. You will be notified of the outcome via email or post. Please allow the full processing time before following up.
What happens if I don't pay or appeal?
03
If you don't pay or appeal within the specified timeframe, the matter may be referred to debt collection agencies or legal action may be taken to recover the amount owed.
Can I call someone about my non-compliance notice?
04
We do not accept appeals via phone to ensure all submissions are properly documented. Please use the official appeals form for any disputes or contact us through our website for general enquiries.
I was visiting a resident or tenant ‚Äî why did I get a non-compliance notice?
05
Visitor parking may require registration through QR codes or permits. Check if you followed the correct visitor registration process displayed at the property. Contact your host about proper visitor parking procedures.
My permit was active ‚Äî why was a non-compliance notice issued?
06
Ensure your permit was properly displayed, valid for the parking area used, and active during your parking period. Technical issues or expired permits may result in notices even if you believe it was active.
I parked in a visitor bay ‚Äî isn't that allowed?
07
Visitor bays often require registration through QR codes or have time restrictions. Check the signage for specific requirements like maximum stay periods or registration procedures.
I parked at a hotel or short-stay property ‚Äî what should I have done?
08
Hotels and short-stay properties may require guest registration or validation. Contact the property directly to understand their parking procedures and whether you need to register your vehicle.
I was delivering or attending a service call ‚Äî am I exempt?
09
Service calls and deliveries may have specific exemptions or procedures. Check if you followed the correct process for service vehicles or if your vehicle qualifies for exemptions under the property's terms.
I scanned the QR code ‚Äî why did I still get a non-compliance notice?
10
QR code registration may have time limits or require complete form submission. Ensure you completed the entire registration process and didn't exceed any time restrictions specified in the system.
Important Notes :
OnlyPark does not issue government fines or infringements. All non-compliance notices are issued in accordance with the terms and conditions of entry displayed onsite at privately managed properties.
